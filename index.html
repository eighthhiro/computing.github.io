Origins of Computing

2700 BC to 1900s

Stretching back to the creation of the Abacus as a simple computing tool. To the transition from mechanical to electromechanical for computing devices.

1937

1st Gen Vacuum Tube Era

Year 1937-1950

The First Digital Computer "ABC" was invented. ABC used vacuum tubes and included binary math. Came along with the inventions Colossus, ENIAC, and EDVAC.

1954

2nd GenTransistor Era

Year 1954-1958

The first transistorized digital computer "TRADIC" saved electrical energy compared to the power-consuming and burning vacuum tubes.

1958

3rd GenIntegrated Circuit Era

Year 1958-present

An Integrated Circuit, now called RAM, is made of transistors packed into a single chip for a more economical and uniform design.

1969

Current Information Age

Year 1969-present

The birth of network computing with the invention of ARPANET AKA "Internet" and the WWW or World Wide Web in 1989.

Influential Figures

Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz

Inventor of Binary

Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (born on July 1, 1646) was widely known for discovering the use of the binary system, which is now the base of all computing systems and operations. Also, Similar to the abacus, he invented the step reckoner, which was used as a calculator design for nearly three centuries.

Charles Babbage

Father of the Computer

Charles Babbage (born December 26, 1791) was a mathematician and inventor who was credited with having conceived the first automatic digital computer. He developed projects such as the Difference and the Analytical Engine, which inspired the first generation of computer scientists who based their works on his inventions.

Ada Lovelace

First Computer Programmer

Ada Lovelace (born December 10, 1815) discovered that a computer could follow a sequence of instructions to make complex calculations, which she then called a program. She wrote hypothetical programs for Babbage's Analytical Engine and, thus, considered the world's first programmer.

Alan Turing

Father of Computer Science

Alan Turing (born June 23, 1912) is the father of computer science and the founding father of artificial intelligence. He theorized that the brain is an unorganized machine, that through training can become a universal machine. Also, he invented the Turing Machine, an abstract computational model that tests the limits of computation.

Herman Hollerith

Founder of IBM

Herman Hollerith (born November 17, 1929) was the founder of IBM and started as a Computing, Tabulating & Recording Company. He invented the Tabulating Machine that helped the US Bureau of Census compile data on population faster. From just an electro-mechanical idea that turned into a leading hardware producer in 1960.
